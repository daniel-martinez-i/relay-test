The Real Opportunity

The opportunity is not replacing nurses.
It's removing the interruptions around care.

Caregivers lose 3+ hours daily to non-clinical tasks—the margin killer no one's fixing.

  • The "21% Rule" (Direct Care Baseline): In current senior care and hospital settings, nurses spend only an estimated 21% of their time on direct patient care. The remaining ~79% is consumed by fragmented administrative and non-clinical logistics.
  • Facility-Level Payroll Exposure: An average 106-bed nursing home carries roughly 247 CNA hours per day. At current median wages, this represents $1.6M to $1.65M in annual CNA payroll-equivalent labor per facility—this is the total pool of labor currently subjected to "fragmentation".
  • The Global Nursing Shortage Scoping: The International Council of Nurses has declared the nursing shortage a "global health emergency," with 13 million to 30.6 million jobs needing replacement.
  • The Automation Target: Research specifically validates that 30% of administrative and support tasks for nurses can be automated or reassigned to robotic systems.

  • Errand Fatigue (The 4-Mile Baseline): Care staff spend a significant portion of their shift purely on "fetching and carrying" tasks (medications, linens, waste), walking at least 4 miles per day purely for these logistical errands.
  • Proven Interruption Reduction: Real-world deployments of delivery robots in clinical settings have already demonstrated a 30% drop in nurse interruptions, significantly improving focus on residents.
  • Specific Throughput Gains: Pilot data shows that automating the "fetch and carry" of the items on your slide (water, linens, supplies) can recover approximately 3.5 hours of staff time per day.
  • The Fall-Risk Safety Loophole: Manual updates of "Fall Risk" signage often lag behind Electronic Health Record (EHR) changes. Relay-style automation ensures 100% compliance in updating room signage within 30 to 45 minutes of an EHR status change, a critical metric for liability-conscious operators.
  • Economic Replacement Logic: In hospital logistics, a single robotic delivery unit currently replaces 3 to 4 full-time equivalents (FTEs), delivering between $180,000 and $300,000 in annual savings.
